Estudy- Your smart study buddy

View live project

Background

eStudy is the approved Exams Preparatory Platform by The West African Examinations Council (WAEC), designed for candidates preparing to sit for the WASSCE (SC/PC/PC2) Examinations. eStudy leverages official past examination questions, multiple-choice, theory-based, learning plans, marking schemes, and performance insights and makes them available as valuable learning content for schools, teachers, and students.

Problem statement

Historically, preparing for WAEC examinations has been a significantly stressful and often overwhelming experience for candidates. Students frequently struggle with the sheer volume of study materials, leading to difficulties in maintaining pace and ultimately impacting their performance. This widespread feeling of being overwhelmed and the challenge of effectively managing study resources hinder many students from achieving their full potential in the WAEC exams.

Project Goal

The project aimed to design eStudy as an interactive, fun, and self-paced learning tool. Its goal is to alleviate the stress of WAEC exam preparation by providing personalized study experiences, comprehensive resources (like mock tests and past questions), and engaging features to improve success rates for students, schools, and sponsors.

Design thinking processes

User research

Conducted semi-structured interviews with a diverse group of 12 WAEC candidates (e.g., recent exam takers, those currently preparing, and those who struggled). Questions focused on their preparation journey, emotional experiences, material management, and preferred learning styles.

Qualitative research

What makes WAEC exam prep challenging for you?

  1. How do you manage study materials, and what overwhelms you?
  2. What study methods work best, and what keeps you motivated?
  3. How do you assess your understanding or readiness?
  4. What key features would you want in a digital WAEC prep platform?
  5. How could a personalized or AI-aided study help you?
  6. What insights on student prep are valuable for schools/sponsors?
